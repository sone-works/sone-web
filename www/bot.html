tone is a music bot for your Discord server that pays artists for streams.

tone offers streaming audio from Soundcloud and Bandcamp to a voice channel in your Discord server. we keep track of plays in aggregate and on a server-by-server basis. this allows us to pay out equitable, transparent, percentage-based payments to artists.

we’re looking for ways to extend the functionality of the bot, further integrate it into other existing platforms and services, and to harness what we’ve learned to continue to build out other useful tools for artists and creatives. if you’re interested in contributing, check out sone and what we’re all about.

commands:

  • /join
    invites tone to your #voice-channel. you must be in the #voice-channel first. 
  • /play [Bandcamp or Soundcloud URL]
    loads your input and adds it to the queue; if there is no playing track, then it will start playing. 
  • /queue
    displays the current song queue. 
  • /skip
    skips the current song.
  • /back
    skips back to the previous song.
  • /clear
    removes all tracks from the queue.
  • /jump [queue position]
    skips to the specified song in the queue. must be a number.
  • /pause
    pauses the current song.
  • /unpause
    unpauses the current song.
  • /remove [song position]
    removes a song from the queue. must be a number. 
  • /shuffle
    toggles shuffling the current queue. disabled by default.
  • /stop
    stops the current song and returns to the beginning of the queue.
  • /song
    displays info on the current song. also accepts 
    /nowplaying
    .
  • /verify [Bandcamp or Soundcloud URL]
    provides a handy URL for initiating the artist account verification and wallet address setup processes.
  • /verified status [Bandcamp or Soundcloud URL]
    checks if tone has confirmed whether an artist has verified their account and connected a wallet address or not.
  • paid tier: 
    /loop
    toggles looping the current queue. enabled by default.
  • paid tier: 
    /info
    toggles song info being displayed at the start of each song. enabled by default.
  • paid tier: 
    /saved queues create [name]
    creates a new saved queue with a unique URL that anyone can use to access and play it from. requires a sndcmpr account.
  • paid tier: 
    /saved queues list
    lists your saved queues. requires a sndcmpr account.
  • paid tier: 
    /saved queues load [sndcmpr URL]
    loads the specified saved queue into the current queue. must be a sndcmpr playlist URL.
  • paid tier: 
    /stats
    shows stats for the top played artists and songs in your server.
  • paid tier: 
    /stats [Bandcamp or Soundcloud URL]
    shows stats for a specific artist in your server.
  • paid tier: 
    /global stats
    shows global stats for the top played artists and songs through tone on all of Discord.
  • paid tier: 
    /global stats [Bandcamp or Soundcloud URL]
    shows global tone stats for a specific artist across all of Discord.
  • paid tier: 
    /scrobble [last.fm username]
    enables last.fm scrobbling for songs played in your server.

pricing:

pay what you can
 
 
 

music: a public good

we think access to music should be a public good. we built tone with that ethos in mind. tone is priced on a sliding scale: simply pay what you can afford on a monthly basis for unlimited plays. as the listener, you set your own price each month and are never turned away, even if you enter $0. tone is a social experiment in altruistic outcomes. we strongly believe, left to regulate itself, a caring and sustainable music community will strive to compensate artists much more than existing major streaming alternatives. every. damn. time.

each month, after hosting fees are paid, all revenue goes toward the following:

  1. 10% - platform fees paid out to Audius, Nina, Bandcamp, etc.,
  2. 10% - to fund future tone development and our public goods treasury,
  3. 80% - artists, via CELO? CELO is digital, global money. it's the currency of Celo apps on the Celo blockchain. we’re using it because it’s incredibly fast, secure, and carbon neutral. to verified artist wallet addresses.

all artist wallet addresses also receive $SONE? $SONE is the token used by sone. it’s used to vote on matters concerning the sone community, and it grants artists and musicians access to various tools and opportunities within sone. we’re just beginning to explore its utility! upon getting verified, which grants access to the sone DAO? Decentralized Autonomous Organization—it’s fancy jargon for a digital co-op, basically. and a growing eco-system of tools (like this one!) funded by our treasury and built by our community.

faq:

  • who built tone?
    tone is a project built and maintained by the sone DAO.
  • does tone support playback from any platforms other than SoundCloud and Bandcamp?
    there are currently no plans to support other platforms. the primary focus of tone is to serve the independent music community.
  • how do artists get paid?
    song plays are tracked on a server-by-server basis. for paid tier Discord servers, plays are totaled each month and payments are split proportionally based on how much of an artist’s music was played within the Discord server (e.g. if Artist X’s music made up 24% of the total played songs in a server within a given month, Artist X would then receive 24% of artist royalties for that server that month).

    tone pays artists in CELO. artists must first set up a wallet and verify their Bandcamp or SoundCloud accounts before they can receive payments. a minimum balance of $10 is required before payment can be claimed and remitted. Check out our stats page if you’re more of a visual person.
  • how can i be sure you’re paying artists?
    all play counts and corresponding payments are publicly available to view.
  • what happens if an artist has a payment balance waiting for them, but they haven’t verified their account or setup a wallet address to get paid?
    artist royalties never expire and are held securely in perpetuity in a multi-sig wallet address until claimed by the artist. maybe you could nudge them and let them know! ;)
  • i’m an artist. how do i verify my Bandcamp and/or SoundCloud account(s)?
    head over to our wallet setup and account verification page.
  • how are subscription fees allocated?
    a non-fixed amount goes to covering hosting expenses incurred for the month.
    10% goes to Bandcamp or SoundCloud as a platform fee (wherever the stream originated from).
    10% goes to the sone DAO treasury to fund further development.
    the remaining amount is split proportionately among artists who had songs streamed in the server within the month.
