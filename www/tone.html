tone is an open-source, platform-agnostic music protocol that enables listeners to curate music from multiple destinations into one unified view. listeners can curate their own cross-platform libraries, create and share cross-platform playlists, and get unlimited access to music on Audius, Bandcamp, Catalog, Nina, Sound, and Zora (with more on the way!) all in one unified view.

we’re looking for ways to extend the functionality of the protocol, further integrate it into other existing platforms and services, and to harness what we’ve learned to continue building other useful tools for artists and creatives. if you’re interested in contributing, check out sone and what we’re all about.

music: a public good

we think access to music should be a public good. we built tone with that ethos in mind. tone is priced on a sliding scale: simply pay what you can afford on a monthly basis for unlimited plays. as the listener, you set your own price each month and are never turned away, even if you enter $0. tone is a social experiment in altruistic outcomes. we strongly believe, left to regulate itself, a caring and sustainable music community will strive to compensate artists much more than existing major streaming alternatives. every. damn. time.

each month, after hosting fees are paid, all revenue goes toward the following:

  1. 10% - platform fees paid out to Audius, Nina, Bandcamp, etc.,
  2. 10% - to fund future tone development and our public goods treasury,
  3. 80% - artists, via CELO? CELO is digital, global money. it's the currency of Celo apps on the Celo blockchain. we’re using it because it’s incredibly fast, secure, and carbon neutral. to verified artist wallet addresses.

all artist wallet addresses also receive $SONE? $SONE is the token used by sone. it’s used to vote on matters concerning the sone community, and it grants artists and musicians access to various tools and opportunities within sone. we’re just beginning to explore its utility! upon getting verified, which grants access to the sone DAO? Decentralized Autonomous Organization—it’s fancy jargon for a digital co-op, basically. and a growing eco-system of tools (like this one!) funded by our treasury and built by our community.

faq:

  • who built tone?
    tone is a project built and maintained by the sone DAO.
  • i’m an artist. how do i sign-up?
    you'll need a web3 profile (aka wallet address). if you have no idea what that is, head to our profile setup guide. otherwise, just select "connect" above.
  • i’m a listener. how do i sign-up?
    you'll need a web3 profile (aka wallet address). if you have no idea what that is, head to our profile setup guide. otherwise, just select "connect" above.
  • what platforms does tone support?
    currently, music can be imported from Audius, Bandcamp, Catalog, Nina, Sound, and Zora with more on the way. there are no plans to integrate with Youtube, Spotify, Apple Music, or any other major DSPs at this time. the primary focus of tone is to serve the independent music community. the platforms we have chosen to support also share this ethos. if you are affiliated with a music platform and would like to integrate with the tone ecosystem, please get in touch!
  • how do artists get paid?
    all song plays are tracked and totaled at the end of each month. after expenses?
    1. non-fixed - hosting costs.
    2. 10% - platform fees paid out to Audius, Nina, Bandcamp, etc..
    3. 10% - future tone development and funding our public goods treasury.
